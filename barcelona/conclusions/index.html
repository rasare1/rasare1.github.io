Conclusions

In conclusion, this research project has allowed us to understand the complex dynamics of air quality in Barcelona. By using professional tools like R Studio and the openair library, we transformed millions of raw data points from the Fabra Observatory into clear, visual evidence. Our analysis shows that pollution is not constant; it follows very specific patterns influenced by human activity and natural factors like the wind and the seasons of the year.

The results from our Time Variation charts reveal that certain pollutants, such as Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2), reach their highest levels during peak traffic hours and colder months. On the other hand, Ozone (O3) shows a completely different behavior, usually increasing during sunny periods. This demonstrates that air quality is a living phenomenon that changes every hour. The use of heatmaps also allowed us to see the long-term evolution of these gases, helping us identify which years were the most critical for public health.

Finally, the integration of meteorological data was the key to this study. We discovered that wind speed and direction play a vital role in cleaning the air or concentrating pollutants in specific areas. This project highlights the importance of data science in environmental protection. Thanks to these techniques, we can move from simple observation to a deeper understanding of our environment, providing the necessary information to take action and improve the air we breathe in our cities.
